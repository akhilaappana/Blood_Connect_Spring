Explore Blood Groups

O+ Blood Group

O+ is the most common blood type in many parts of the world. It contains red blood cells, plasma, white blood cells, and platelets. O+ individuals can donate blood to anyone with O+, A+, B+, and AB+ blood types. They can receive blood from O+, O-, A+, A-, B+, B-, AB+, and AB- blood types. O+ blood is essential for a wide range of medical conditions, including trauma care, surgery, and chronic illnesses. It is also important for pregnant women and babies with blood incompatibilities. Regular donation of O+ blood can help save lives in emergencies.

Who Can Donate?

People with type O+ can donate blood to O+, A+, B+, and AB+ blood types.

Who Can Receive?

People with O+ can receive blood from O+, O-, A+, A-, B+, B-, AB+, and AB- blood types.

O- Blood Group

O- is the universal blood donor type. This rare blood type is essential in emergency situations, as it can be given to anyone. O- blood is especially valuable in trauma cases and surgeries, as it is compatible with all other blood types (O-, O+, A-, A+, B-, B+, AB-, and AB+). However, O- individuals can only receive blood from other O- donors. It is essential for O- blood donors to maintain healthy iron levels and general health for frequent donations. O- blood plays a critical role in saving lives, particularly in hospitals and trauma centers.

Who Can Donate?

People with type O- can donate blood to O-, O+, A-, A+, B-, B+, AB-, and AB+ blood types.

Who Can Receive?

People with O- can receive blood from O- blood type.

A+ Blood Group

Blood type A+ is characterized by the presence of the A antigen and the Rh factor. It contains red blood cells, white blood cells, platelets, and plasma. People with A+ blood can donate to individuals with A+ and AB+ blood types, and can receive blood from A+, A-, O+, and O- blood types. This blood type is essential for treating patients undergoing surgeries, recovering from trauma, or managing chronic illnesses like anemia. Since it contains specific antibodies, individuals with A+ blood may experience allergic reactions to incompatible blood transfusions. It's important to ensure a healthy lifestyle, as blood donors with A+ blood are encouraged to stay hydrated, eat iron-rich foods, and avoid any health conditions that may affect their blood cells. Blood donations from A+ individuals are highly sought after in hospitals to treat cancer patients, trauma victims, and those undergoing surgery.

Who Can Donate?

People with type A+ can donate blood to A+ and AB+ blood types.

Who Can Receive?

People with A+ can receive blood from A+, A-, O+, and O- blood types.

A- Blood Group

Blood type A- is similar to A+, but without the Rh factor. It contains red blood cells, white blood cells, platelets, and plasma, and is important for blood transfusions in surgeries, emergencies, and cancer treatments. A- individuals can donate blood to A-, A+, AB-, and AB+ blood types, and can only receive blood from A- and O- blood types. A- is a rare blood type, making it a crucial resource in hospitals, especially in cases that require rare blood matching. As with all blood types, maintaining good health and a balanced diet, including iron-rich foods, is important for those with A- blood.

Who Can Donate?

People with type A- can donate blood to A-, A+, AB-, and AB+ blood types.

Who Can Receive?

People with A- can receive blood from A- and O- blood types.

B+ Blood Group

B+ blood contains the B antigen and the Rh factor. It can donate blood to B+ and AB+ recipients, and can receive blood from B+, B-, O+, and O- blood types. This blood type is used for a variety of medical treatments, including trauma cases, surgeries, and cancer treatments. It's also important for treating patients with blood diseases and those undergoing chemotherapy. For those with B+ blood, maintaining a healthy lifestyle is essential to ensure a continuous, healthy supply of blood cells for donation.

Who Can Donate?

People with type B+ can donate blood to B+ and AB+ blood types.

Who Can Receive?

People with B+ can receive blood from B+, B-, O+, and O- blood types.

B- Blood Group

Blood type B- is similar to B+ but lacks the Rh factor. It is relatively rare, making it a critical resource in medical settings. Like B+, B- contains red blood cells, white blood cells, platelets, and plasma, all of which are vital for various medical treatments. B- blood can be given to B-, B+, AB-, and AB+ recipients, and B- individuals can receive blood only from B- and O- donors. This blood type is especially important in emergency transfusions and surgeries.

Who Can Donate?

People with type B- can donate blood to B-, B+, AB-, and AB+ blood types.

Who Can Receive?

People with B- can receive blood from B- and O- blood types.

AB+ Blood Group

AB+ is a universal plasma donor, meaning that people with AB+ blood can donate plasma to anyone. This blood type contains both A and B antigens, along with the Rh factor. AB+ individuals can donate blood to AB+ recipients and can receive blood from all blood types. It is particularly useful in trauma, surgeries, and cancer treatments. Because AB+ is less common, it is critical for medical professionals to ensure a proper match when administering transfusions to patients.

Who Can Donate?

People with type AB+ can donate blood to AB+ blood type.

Who Can Receive?

People with AB+ can receive blood from all blood types.

AB- Blood Group

AB- is a rare blood type that contains both A and B antigens but lacks the Rh factor. AB- can donate blood to AB- and AB+ recipients, and it can receive blood from AB-, AB+, A-, A+, B-, B+, O-, and O+ blood types. This blood type is especially important for trauma cases, cancer treatments, and surgeries where the immune system needs to be supported. As AB- is a rare blood type, those with this blood type should be vigilant about their health, avoid overexertion, and maintain a balanced diet. This blood type is useful for complex medical procedures that require precise blood matching.

Who Can Donate?

People with type AB- can donate blood to AB- and AB+ blood types.

Who Can Receive?

People with AB- can receive blood from AB-, AB+, A-, A+, B-, B+, O-, and O+ blood types.
